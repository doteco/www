Once .eco goes live to the public in the new year, securing names will become competitive and could be expensive. Who can apply for a .eco domain grant? The Domain Grant program is open to any environment NGO, non-profit or government for whom environmental protection is a central mandate. You can also apply if your organization is a member of a recognized coalition with an environmental mission. How many names can my organization apply for? Each organization or individual can submit up to three applications for one name each. To help us ensure everyone gets a name they want, please rank your names in order of preference. What criteria does .eco use to award domain grants? The primary criteria .eco is using to award domain grants are: - Relevance of your proposal. When a user visits your granted .eco domain, the site content is relevant to the keyword and supports your org’s purpose and environmental mission. Ask yourself, is this what the average internet user would expect to find here? - Scope. Your project has reach and impact aligned with your goals. - Diversity. It is important to us that the cohort of .eco domain grant recipients be geographically and culturally diverse and represent a variety of environmental issue areas. Where there are multiple strong applications for the same name, governments will be granted the domain first, followed by a non-profit. Oldest non-profits, by founding date, will be offered the name before newly-founded organizations. Learn more about these policy guidelines designed by the .eco council. When will I find out if my organizations receives a domain grant? The initial phase of applications for domain grants close at the end of November and grants will be awarded in January, 2016. When can I use my .eco domain? If you receive a domain grant in December, you can start using your domain in May, 2017. Will I be able to run a .eco domain and another web domain name at the same time? Yes. Having a .eco name doesn’t mean you have to give up your other sites. It can be used for a specific campaign, or be a place for a particular project. Do I get to keep my granted .eco domain name forever? Approved .eco keyword grants remain in the pool of environmental community grant names forever. Grantees will have 12 months from May 1, 2017 to launch something on their .eco domain name. If your grant name is not in use inside the 12-month activation window, we’ll be in touch to discuss a plan for your use of the granted domain name(s) or returning them to the community. As long as names are being actively used, there is no limit on the duration of the grant. In other words, if you’re actively using your .eco domain, it’s yours forever. But, if you stop using your granted .eco domain, it will be returned to the sandbox so that other non-profits can apply to use that .eco domain name. Will .eco give back to the environmental community? Yes. We will create independent foundation that will allocate a significant portion of the sales of .eco names to help the environment. You can read more about our history and commitment to the environmental community. Importantly, environmental groups will play a key role in making sure .eco funds are well spent.
