Non-profits get early access to reserve .eco domains

Apply to reserve your .eco domain before April 2, 2017

.eco is a new top-level domain created by and for the environmental community.

Endorsed by WWF, Greenpeace and NRDC, it’s a trusted, verified symbol for organizations, businesses and people committed to positive change for the planet.

Until April 2, 2017, environmental non-profits get early access to reserve .eco domains for trade names. A trade name is any version of your organization’s name—it could be your official name, an acronym or the name your organization commonly goes by.

For example, if your current URL is NorthWestWatershedAlliance.org, you can apply to reserve: NorthWestWatershedAlliance.eco, NWWA.eco or WatershedAlliance.eco.

Reserve your name now

Once domain names go on sale to the public on April 18, 2017, you will be able to purchase approved trade names.

*If you have an active trademark and want to ensure you protect it by securing the relevant .eco domain(s), we can help you access the ICANN Trademark Clearinghouse. Email [email address] if this is of interest to you.

Apply to reserve your .eco trade name domain if

Your organization is a not-for-profit, charity or government and environmental work is central to your mission
